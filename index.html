01 — About

Who I Am

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in Data Science in Bioeconomy at the Leibniz Institute for Agricultural Engineering and Bioeconomy (ATB) in Potsdam, Germany. My research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, bioprocess engineering, and sustainable production systems.

As a Scientific Researcher, I specialize in food and bioprocess analytics, monitoring, and decision-making evaluation techniques, leveraging the power of AI for real-world applications. My skills include machine learning, modeling, simulation, operations research and optimization of food manufacturing environments and biosystems.

I am driven to connect and explore opportunities for advancing knowledge in sustainable resource consumption, creating a positive impact on our Environment and Industry. With many publications, hundreds of citations, and a strong readership, I contribute actively to advancing the bioeconomy research frontier.

02 — Research Prospects

Research Areas & Future Directions

🧬

Continual Adaptive Learning for Food and Bioprocess Monitoring

Developing next-generation real-time monitoring systems using fluorescence spectroscopy, machine learning, and transfer learning to eliminate costly offline sampling in food and bioprocesses.

Domain AdaptationTransfer LearningOut-of-Distribution
☕

Food Authenticity & Quality Analytics

Building spectroscopy-based AI systems (NIR, Raman, 2D fluorescence, Hyperspectral images) to detect process anomaly, food adulteration and ensure quality in food and other agricultural products.

AuthenticitySpectroscopyAdulteration
🌐

Digital Twins for Biosystems

Creating biosystem-informed digital twin models that integrate mechanistic models with data-driven AI to simulate and optimize complex food drying, fermentation, and other biosystems.

AI for Sustainable Bioeconomy

Leveraging data science and machine learning across the entire bioeconomy value chain — from biomass production to biobased chemicals — to support evidence-based decision making for a greener economy.
