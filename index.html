About

I am a researcher working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, food engineering, and bioprocess systems.

My work focuses on developing data-driven monitoring and decision frameworks that improve efficiency, quality, and sustainability in complex production environments.

I am particularly interested in bridging scientific models with real-world industrial applications across the bioeconomy.

Research

AI methods for adaptive monitoring of food and bioprocess systems using spectroscopy and real-time data.
Data-driven approaches for food quality analysis and authenticity assessment.
Digital twin models integrating simulation and machine learning for biosystems.
Optimization of production systems using operations research and scheduling.
